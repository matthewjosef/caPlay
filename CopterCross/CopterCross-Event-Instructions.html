Pilot flies Copter. Each person will take turns playing both roles. Find a buddy team! These are your go-to people for questions, problems etc. They will also be the first team that you compete against during your skill builder exercizes. Plus, during the main event, they are going to be your partners in the relay race. You will compete against them. Then you will compete with them. 2) INTRO TO THE EQUIPMENT QUADCOPTER (need a diagram. copy from instructions) 1) Body 2) Arms 3) Motors 4) Rotors 5) Rotor Guard 6) QC Power Cable 7) Battery and Battery Cable CONTROLLER (need a diagram. copy from instructions) 1) On/Off switch 2) Elevator 3) Throttle 4) Elevator Trim 5) Throttle Trim 6) Screen etc. 3) SAFETY THIRD! BLADES At these speeds, blades are sharp! Do not fly near other people. Do not try to pick up copter while blades are spinning. Do not try to catch copter in the air. Rotor guards help minimize the likelihood of injury How to pick up a Copter without hurting it or yourself a) Pilot (P): Move elevator to the down position, then say: ["ELEVATOR DOWN"] b) P: Set down the controller, then say: ["CONTROLLER DOWN"] c) P: Take hands off controller, then say: ["GOOD TO GRAB"] d) Co-Pilot (CP): Say ["GRABBING!]" e) CP: Grab QuadCopter BY THE BODY. Not by the arms. Not by the rotors. BATTERIES Batteries only last for 5-7 minutes of flight time. You will have to change batteries repeatedly during this exercise. Use the Battery Changing Cheat Sheet each time. 1) Turn Controller OFF 2) DETACHING a battery from the QuadCopter A) Breath! B) Be gentle. If you are too rough here, you can strip the connector off of the electrical wires, thus breaking the quadcopter and disqualifying your team from the race. C) Pinch on the edges of the connection and carefully pull the connection apart. 3) Place used battery on Charger. Make sure that the RED charging light turns on. If the charger has no room for the battery (or if the event ends before batteries are fully charged), place the un(der)-charged batteries in the RED BAG. 4) Move any extra FULLY CHARGED batteries from the CHARGER to the GREEN BAG. 5) Take one FULLY CHARGED battery out of the green bag for use in the QuadCopter. 6) CONNECTING a battery to the QuadCopter 1) Breathe! 2) Be gentle. If you are too rough here, you can bend the pins on the connection, thus breaking the quad copter, and disqualify your team from the race.
